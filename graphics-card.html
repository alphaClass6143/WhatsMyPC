Graphics card

What is it and what does it do?

The graphics card or GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) is the component responsible for creating the image on the screen. The GPU calculates the different colors and shapes on the screen and then sends these informations to the display to show it.

Where is it?

The graphics card can be visible as a separate rather big component slotted in under the processor or it can be integrated into the processor. Sizes can vary.

Additional info

Graphics cards can be one of the most expensive components in a PC. Most basic units integrated into the processor are well enough for day to day use. Games and graphical renders like videos, images or 3D object, can have a need for a good graphics card that can take up to 50% of the overall PC cost.
