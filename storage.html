Storage

What is it and what does it do?

The power supply or PSU (Power Supply Unit), is supplying power to all the components in the PC. It converts the power from the outlet to usable DC current for the computer. There are several cables going to different parts, mainly the storage, motherboard and graphics card.

Where is it?

The location of the storage depends heavily on the type of storage. HDDs and normal SSDs are mostly kept in drive bays around the case. A hard drive can be located by its two cables from the back. One cable goes to the power supply, the other one to the motherboard. There are also card like SSDs (M.2 drives) which are slotted in on the motherboard. The position on the motherboard can vary a lot depending on the manufacturer.
